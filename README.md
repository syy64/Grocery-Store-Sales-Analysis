# Grocery Store Sales & Performance Analysis

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes sales performance, product categories, outlet types, and customer preferences within a grocery retail environment. The goal is to uncover key drivers of revenue, product demand, and outlet performance to support data-driven business decisions.

## 🎯 Business Objective
To evaluate sales trends, product performance, and outlet efficiency, and identify opportunities to optimize revenue and customer satisfaction.

## 📊 Key Metrics
- Total Sales: $1.20M  
- Average Sales per Item: $140.99  
- Total Number of Items Sold: 2,000+  
- Average Customer Rating: 3.96  

---

## 🔍 Sales & Product Insights

### 🛒 Item Category Performance
- Top-performing categories include:
  - Fruits & Vegetables  
  - Snack Foods  
  - Household Items  
- Lower-performing categories:
  - Seafood  
  - Breakfast  
  - Starchy Foods  

**Insight:**  
Essential and frequently consumed goods drive the highest revenue, indicating demand is strongest for everyday consumables.

---

### 🥑 Fat Content Impact
- Low Fat Products:
  - Sales: $0.78M  
- Regular Products:
  - Sales: $0.43M  

**Insight:**  
Low-fat products significantly outperform regular products, suggesting a strong customer preference for healthier options.

---

## 🏪 Outlet Performance Analysis

### 🏬 Outlet Type Performance
- Supermarket Type 1 generates the highest overall sales  
- Grocery stores contribute less compared to supermarket formats  

**Insight:**  
Larger retail formats drive higher sales due to wider product availability and better customer reach.

---

### ⏳ Outlet Age Impact
- Older outlets tend to generate higher sales  

**Insight:**  
Established stores benefit from customer loyalty and brand familiarity.

---

## 📊 Customer Behavior Insights

### ⭐ Ratings vs Sales
- Average rating is relatively high (3.96) across all products  
- Minimal variation between product categories  

**Insight:**  
Customer satisfaction is stable, but not a strong differentiator of sales performance.

---

## ⚖️ Combined Insights

### Fat Content vs Outlet Type
- Low-fat products dominate across all outlet types (~64% of sales)  

**Insight:**  
Health-conscious purchasing behavior is consistent regardless of store format.

---

## ⚠️ Key Findings
- Sales are heavily driven by essential product categories  
- Low-fat products dominate revenue contribution  
- Supermarket formats outperform smaller grocery stores  
- Older outlets generate stronger revenue  
- Customer ratings remain stable across categories  

---

## ✅ Recommendations

### 1. Expand High-Demand Categories
- Increase inventory for fruits, snacks, and household items  
- Prioritize supply chain efficiency for fast-moving goods  

### 2. Leverage Health Trends
- Promote low-fat products through targeted marketing  
- Expand healthy product lines  

### 3. Optimize Store Strategy
- Invest more in supermarket-type outlets  
- Replicate successful strategies from high-performing locations  

### 4. Strengthen Underperforming Categories
- Improve visibility and promotion of low-performing products  
- Re-evaluate pricing or product quality
